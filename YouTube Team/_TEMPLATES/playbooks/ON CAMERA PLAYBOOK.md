## **Core Principle**

Cameras flatten your energy.  
Speak at **7 / 10 intensity** to look natural on screen.

---

## **PART 1 — FOUNDATION (Weeks 1–4)**

### **1. Find Your Voice**

- Describe your natural communication in 3–5 words.
    
- Ask 3 friends for one-word summaries; find overlap.
    
- Choose an energy that fits your niche:
    
    - Calm = deep explainers
        
    - Energetic = news/AI updates
        

### **2. Remove Voice & Appearance Anxiety**

- Voice sounds strange due to bone conduction—keep editing yourself.
    
- Camera 3–4 ft away, mirror mode off.
    
- Treat discomfort as a technical adjustment period (2–4 weeks).
    

### **3. Embrace Productive Imperfection**

- Keep natural “thinking” moments.
    
- Avoid rambling or major factual slips.
    
- Credibility = expertise + honesty.
    

### **4. Reframe the Camera**

- Picture one real person behind the lens.
    
- Practice on Zoom or send short video messages.
    

---

## **PART 2 — CORE TECHNIQUES (Weeks 2–4)**

### **Warm-Up Routine (6–9 min)**

1. Play energetic music (2–3 min)
    
2. Be silly or act lines (2–3 min)
    
3. Sing to camera (2–3 min)
    

### **Bullets > Scripts**

Make 5–7 bullet points. Speak freely. Re-record only for clarity.

### **Body & Voice**

- Lean in; keep forearms visible.
    
- Use hand gestures inside the **torso box** (shoulders–waist).
    
- Look at lens, not screen.
    
- Smile lightly; vary gesture width with energy.
    

### **Delivery Control**

- Pause when rushing.
    
- **Clap or snap** before re-starting sentences (helps editing).
    
- “Think aloud” on tough concepts to sound real.

## **PART 3 — IMPROVEMENT LOOP**

### **TEACH Framework (Self-Review)**

| T | Tone | Confident? Fitting to topic? |  
| E | Eye Contact | Lens-focused, natural blinks? |  
| A | Animation | Using hands naturally? |  
| C | Coherence | Clear structure, examples? |  
| H | Haste | Speed right? Pauses used? |

→ Watch every video twice, note 1–2 fixes.

### **Feedback Ask**

> “If you had to give me ONE piece of advice to improve this, what would it be?”  
> Track repeating patterns; ignore one-offs.

### **Continuous Loop**

**Create → Analyze → Get Feedback → Adjust → Repeat**  
10 videos = visible transformation.

## **PART 4 — SKILL DRILLS (Weeks 5–8)**

|Drill|Frequency|Goal|How-To|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Rapid Fire Autocomplete**|3× / week (10 min)|Kill hesitation|Finish random prompts in 3–6 s. Round 1 speed → Round 2 + gestures → Round 3 + 7/10 energy.|
|**Everyday Drill**|Daily (10 min)|Natural flow|Talk about emotional parts of your day (“felt stuck when…”).|
|**Triple Step**|2× / week (15 min)|Stay coherent under chaos|Random words appear → link them to your topic. Recover with “What I mean is …”|
|**Energy Game**|1–2× / week (15 min)|Energy control|Partner calls numbers 1–10 → instantly match posture, volume, tone.|
|**Autocomplete + Rest**|2× / week (15 min)|Build short speeches|After 3–5 prompts, deliver 30–60 s story with one example or opinion.|
|**Blindfold Slideshow**|1× / week (15 min)|Adaptability|Random slides 10–15 s each. Relate every image to one core message.|
|**Imitation Game**|1× / week (20 min)|Range & style|Copy styles of 3–5 creators (Neistat, Harris, Abdaal, 3B1B). Blend what fits you.|
All drills train comfort, spontaneity, and structure.  
Here’s what to _actually do_ in each.

---

### **1. Rapid Fire Autocomplete (3×/week, 10 min)**

**Goal:** Kill hesitation & overthinking.  
**How:**

1. Use random sentence starters like “The biggest problem with AI is…”
    
2. Finish in 3–6 seconds.
    
3. Round 1 = pure speed (3s).
    
4. Round 2 = add gestures (5s).
    
5. Round 3 = full energy (6s).  
    20–30 prompts per session.
    

---

### **2. Everyday Drill (Daily, 10 min)**

**Goal:** Build natural, emotional delivery.  
**How:**

1. Turn camera on — no plan, no script.
    
2. Talk about your **day’s emotional moments**, not events.
    
    > “I was frustrated with my code because…”
    
3. Week 1: Just talk.  
    Week 2: Add emotion.  
    Week 3: Control energy.  
    Week 4: Tell one full story arc.
    

---

### **3. Triple Step Drill (2×/week, 15 min)**

**Goal:** Stay coherent while distracted.  
**How:**

- Use random word generator while speaking about a topic.
    
- Every 6–10 seconds, a new random word pops up.
    
- Seamlessly link it to your main idea.
    
- If lost: “What I’m really saying is…” and return to point.  
    Progressively reduce time between prompts each week.
    

---

### **4. Energy Game (1–2×/week, 15 min)**

**Goal:** Learn to modulate energy on command.  
**How:**

1. Partner/app shouts energy level 1–10 every 10–15 seconds.
    
2. Adjust instantly:
    
    - 1–3 → quiet, close posture
        
    - 4–6 → conversational, neutral
        
    - 7–10 → big gestures, louder tone
        
3. Practice storytelling while shifting energy.
    

---

### **5. Autocomplete with Rest (2×/week, 15 min)**

**Goal:** Build mini-speeches fast.  
**How:**

1. Do 3–5 quick autocomplete prompts.
    
2. Then stop and deliver a **30-second speech** on the last topic.
    
3. Think for 5 seconds, then speak — include one personal story or example.
    
4. Progress: 30s → 45s → 60s speeches over weeks.
    

---

### **6. Blindfold Slideshow (1×/week, 15 min)**

**Goal:** Stay calm and creative under chaos.  
**How:**

1. Random slideshow of 10–15 images (you don’t see them before).
    
2. Choose a topic like “Future of AI.”
    
3. Pick one **core message** (e.g., “AI helps, but judgment stays human”).
    
4. As slides change every 10–15s, connect each image metaphorically to your message.
    
5. End with a clear conclusion.  
    If stuck: “This reminds me of…” → return to core message.
    

---

### **7. Imitation Game (1×/week, 20 min)**

**Goal:** Expand delivery range & find your own fusion style.  
**How:**

1. Pick 3–5 distinct creators (e.g., Casey Neistat, Johnny Harris, Ali Abdaal, 3Blue1Brown).
    
2. Watch a short clip from each.
    
3. Immediately imitate their **tone, pacing, and body language** on a new topic.
    
4. After all, combine what felt authentic into your “fusion” style.

## **PART 5 — ADVANCED MENTAL MODELS**

### **Two Archetypes**

|Archetype|Use When|Style|
|---|---|---|
|**Classroom Presenter**|Tutorials, model explainers|Firm tone, open gestures, 7/10 energy|
|**Therapist**|Personal stories, struggles|Low energy (3–4/10), slower pace, empathy|

Switch between them within a video for contrast.

### **Wingspan Rule**

Gesture width = energy:

- Narrow (1–3/10) = intimate
    
- Torso box (4–6/10) = neutral
    
- Wide (7–9/10) = impact moments
    

### **Chest-Fly Posture**

Open chest → shoulders back → head up. Confidence looks and feels real.

### **5-Second Transformation Story**

> “I used to think X → then Y happened → now I think Z.”  
> Use for quick, memorable narratives.

### **Three Breakthroughs**

1. **Personal stories > polish** – use mini-stories in technical videos.
    
2. **Physicality projects confidence** – open posture + visible hands.
    
3. **Strong finishes signal authority** – end key points with firm tone and downward inflection.

## **PART 6 — SUSTAINABLE PRACTICE**

### **Training Plans**

|Level|Weekly Time|Structure|
|---|---|---|
|**Minimum**|30 min|Everyday Drill × 3|
|**Standard**|1.5–2 hrs|Mix of Everyday + Rapid Fire + Energy Game + TEACH review|
|**Accelerated**|3–4 hrs|All drills + weekly TEACH + integration video|

### **Reduce Friction**

Keep tripod + phone always ready. Start recording in < 2 min.

### **Accountability**

Partner or public 10-video commitment; add light stakes if needed.

### **Progress Tracking**

Spreadsheet: Date | Drill | Duration | Lesson | Next Focus.  
Weekly Sunday review → adjust.

### **Monthly Integration**

1. Record 10–15 min video.
    
2. TEACH review + external feedback.
    
3. Compare to last month; set 2 new focus areas.
