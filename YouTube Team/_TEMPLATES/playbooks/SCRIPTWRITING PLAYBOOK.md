### _The System to Research, Write, and Polish YouTube Scripts That Actually Perform_

### **1. THE FOUNDATION: RESEARCH IS 80% OF YOUR SCRIPT**

If you start writing before researching, you’re scripting blind.

Bryan’s framework has **4 pillars**:

#### **(1) Audience Understanding**

Know _who you’re writing for_ and _what they obsess over_.

- What keeps them stuck?
    
- What do they _believe_ that’s wrong?
    
- What’s the transformation they want?

**Action Steps**

- Read **5–10 comment sections** under top videos in your niche.
    
- List the **top 10 repeated questions or complaints**.
    
- Turn each question into a video idea.

**Example (AI Trading Channel):**

> Comment: “AI models never work in real markets.”  
> Script Angle: “I tested AI in live trading for 30 days — here’s what really happened.”

---

#### **(2) Competitor Analysis**

You’re not guessing — you’re reverse-engineering what’s working.

**Action Steps**

1. Pick **3–5 high-performing videos** on the same topic.
    
2. Note:
    
    - Title + thumbnail style
        
    - Hook format (first 15 seconds)
        
    - Retention peaks (use vidIQ or YouTube Studio)
        
    - Comment section requests (“I wish they showed…”)
        

**Example Note:**

> Competitor Video: “How to Beat the Market Using AI”  
> Retention peaks at minute 3 when he shares his code.  
> Drop-off after 6 mins when he starts theory — means action > abstraction.

---

#### **(3) Gather Content**

You need _original material_ your competitors don’t have.

**Action Steps**

- Read 2–3 articles, whitepapers, or case studies outside YouTube.
    
- Pull unique data, examples, or visuals.
    
- Add your personal test, experiment, or story.
    

**Example:**

> “I found a 2024 Stanford paper on LSTM models — and backtested it myself. The results were nothing like the paper claimed.”

---

#### **(4) Validate**

Don’t trust your memory. Check everything.

**Action Steps**

- Fact-check every number, quote, or result.
    
- Ask: _Does this point directly help my viewer achieve their goal?_
    
- Cut anything that doesn’t add **value per minute**.
    

---

### **2. FINDING THE PERFECT VIDEO LENGTH**

Bryan’s rule:

> “The more minutes you take, the more value you must give.”

|Format|Length|Use Case|Example|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Short**|2–5 min|Quick tips / comparisons|“3 AI tools to predict stock moves”|
|**Medium**|6–12 min|Tutorials / frameworks|“How to build a simple LSTM model for trading”|
|**Long**|15–30 min|Deep dives / case studies|“I built an AI hedge fund simulator from scratch”|

**Action Steps**

1. Analyze top videos in your niche → average their runtimes.
    
2. Choose your format → script that time +/– 10%.
    
3. Measure retention → aim for **65%+ average view duration**.
    

---

### **3. PICKING THE RIGHT POINTS AND PAYOFFS**

Your **payoffs** are the main takeaways — they’re why viewers stay.

**Common Mistakes**

- Covering too many ideas → no depth.
    
- Talking about what _you_ like → not what _they_ need.
    
- Repeating what everyone already knows.
    

**Action Steps**

1. Write 10 points you could cover.
    
2. Cut until you have 3–5 that:
    
    - Answer your audience’s _specific pain_.
        
    - Haven’t been overdone.
        
    - Build toward a transformation or result.
        

**Example (AI Trading):**  
❌ “AI can predict stock prices.”  
✅ “Here’s how I trained an AI to predict Tesla’s price — and how accurate it actually was.”

---

### **4. STANDING OUT FROM THE CROWD**

You can’t out-edit the algorithm, but you can out-research everyone.

**Action Steps**

- Post a poll: “What confuses you most about trading with AI?”
    
- Check analytics: find your biggest retention drop and rewrite that section.
    
- Track hook performance:
    
    - Version A (story hook)
        
    - Version B (stat hook)
        
    - Compare first 30-sec retention.
        

**Example Hook Test:**

- A: “AI traders make money while they sleep — or do they?”
    
- B: “I trained ChatGPT to beat the market — and it failed miserably.”  
    → Keep whichever one gets 50%+ retention in the first 30 seconds.
    

---

### **5. THE VIDEO GRADING SYSTEM**

When researching or revising, grade yourself like Bryan does.

|Category|What to Check|Example of a 3/3|
|---|---|---|
|**Insight**|Are ideas unique?|“Backtested an unpublished AI paper.”|
|**Hook**|Strong first 15 sec?|“This $20 model beat ChatGPT in trading.”|
|**Pacing**|Smooth flow, no drag?|Tight edits, short lines, 5–7s rhythm.|
|**Tonality**|Energy matches topic?|Calm, confident, not hypey for finance.|
|**Storytelling**|Emotion + clarity?|“I lost $200 testing this, but learned why models fail.”|
|**Editing**|Visuals fit pacing?|Each scene change = new insight or data.|

**Action Step**

- Grade 3 competitor videos before every new upload.
    
- Write 3 improvements to over-index on.
    

---

### **6. MASTERING HOOKS (BRYAN’S SIGNATURE FRAMEWORK)**

**Purpose:** The first 30 seconds decide the next 10 minutes.  
Your hook must:

- Confirm the click (prove the title wasn’t clickbait)
    
- Add curiosity
    
- Set stakes
    
- Show uniqueness
    

---

#### **10 TYPES OF HOOKS (With Examples)**

|Type|Example|
|---|---|
|**1. Question**|“Can AI really predict the next stock market crash?”|
|**2. Shocking Statement**|“90% of AI trading bots lose money — here’s why mine didn’t.”|
|**3. Storytelling**|“Two months ago, I built an AI trader that outperformed my mentor.”|
|**4. Preview**|“I’ll show you the exact steps to build and test your first AI stock model.”|
|**5. Personal Connection**|“If you’ve ever watched your trades fail while an algorithm wins, this video is for you.”|
|**6. Statistic/Fact**|“Traders using AI earn 27% higher returns — but 80% still lose money.”|
|**7. Challenge**|“Give me 5 minutes, and I’ll prove AI can trade better than humans.”|
|**8. Quotation**|“Warren Buffett warned about this — and AI just made it worse.”|
|**9. Metaphor**|“AI trading is like poker: you win by folding most of the time.”|
|**10. Proof**|“Here’s the live dashboard showing how my AI picked the top 3 stocks this month.”|

**Action Steps**

- Write 3 hook versions before scripting.
    
- Speak them out loud — the one that sounds natural wins.
    
- Test it: if it doesn’t keep **40–50% retention in the first 30 seconds**, rewrite it.
    

---

### **7. HOW TO WRITE A STRONG INTRO**

Your intro should:

1. Validate the click (restate title in story form)
    
2. Introduce stakes (why this matters now)
    
3. Preview transformation (what they’ll learn)
    

**Example:**

> “I built an AI to trade stocks for me.  
> Week one: it lost $200.  
> Week three: it beat my manual trades by 32%.  
> Here’s everything I changed to make that happen.”

**Action Steps**

- Use your **best point second**, not first.
    
- End intro with a cliffhanger: “But there was one problem that almost ruined it…”
    

---

### **8. WRITING THE BODY**

Bryan’s rule: _Every line should pull the viewer to the next._

**Action Steps**

1. For every point:
    
    - Give context
        
    - Show example
        
    - Add “why this matters”
        
    - Open a small loop for next section
        
2. Cut transitions that don’t add curiosity.
    
3. Add a _visual cue_ or stat every 20–30 seconds.
    

**Example:**

> “This model looked perfect — until I realized it was overfitting.  
> Here’s how I fixed that in under 10 lines of code.”

---

### **9. END STRONG — PAYOFF & CTA**

Never fade out. Finish with clarity and direction.

**Structure:**

1. Recap transformation
    
2. Reward insight (“Now you know why AI trading fails — and how to fix it”)
    
3. Call to action naturally
    
    - “If you want the full code, it’s in the next video.”
        
    - “I built a free version of this AI model — link below.”
        

**Action Steps**

- Record the outro right after the intro. It keeps your tone consistent.
    
- Never end on “Thanks for watching.” End on a hook for the next video.

### **Final Thought**

Bryan’s entire method rests on one truth:

> “If your first 30 seconds don’t hit, nothing else matters.”

Your script is a product. Treat it like a product launch — research, test, and iterate until every line sells the next one.
