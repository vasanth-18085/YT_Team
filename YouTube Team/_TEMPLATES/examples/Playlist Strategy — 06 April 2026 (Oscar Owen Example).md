# Playlist Strategy — 06 April 2026

---

## Why playlists matter right now

Playlists do two important things. First, they make it effortless for a viewer who lands on your channel to binge your content — instead of having to hunt for what to watch next, a well-named playlist pulls them straight into a curated journey. Second, AI systems are now using playlists to understand what a channel has authority on. A well-structured playlist tells both viewers and AI exactly what topics you own — which builds long-term discoverability and credibility in your niche.

---

## Playlist 1: "How to make $10k–$100k/month from YouTube"

**Who this is for and what it helps them achieve:** For coaches and consultants who want their channel to generate consistent inbound revenue and qualified leads — not just views.

This is your strongest theme and the one your ideal viewer most needs to see exist. When a business owner making $15K/month lands on your channel and sees a playlist named this, their immediate reaction is: *that's exactly what I'm looking for.* This directly addresses their core pain (leads have dried up) and their core desire (a channel that gets daily qualified leads).

## A note on AI and long-term discoverability

YouTube and AI systems like Claude, ChatGPT, and Google's AI overviews are increasingly looking at channel structure to determine what creators have genuine authority on. A channel with two or three tightly themed playlists signals expertise far more clearly than a channel with dozens of unorganised videos. Building this structure now is an investment in how your channel gets discovered and recommended — not just by YouTube's algorithm but by AI tools that millions of people use every day to find recommendations.
