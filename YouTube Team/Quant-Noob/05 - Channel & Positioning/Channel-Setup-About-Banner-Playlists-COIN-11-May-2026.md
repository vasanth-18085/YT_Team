---

> **The Quant Noob** is me — Vasanth — a software engineer with an ML background teaching myself quant finance in public.
>
> I'm not a quant yet. That's the point.
>
> This channel documents the full journey: studying the CPF (Certificate in Python and Finance), working through the EPAT, building toward the MScFE from World Quant University, and building real quant finance projects along the way. All of it — the concepts that clicked, the ones that didn't, the exam results, the failed backtests.
>
> If you're a software engineer or ML person who's been curious about quant finance but hasn't found a channel built for people with a technical background who are starting from zero — this is it.
>
> What you'll find here:
> — Concept explainers for quant math and statistics (coded, not just theorized)
> — Honest reviews of CPF, EPAT, and quant finance books
> — Build-in-public sessions where I code quant projects live
> — Monthly updates: what I studied, what clicked, what didn't
> — Study livestreams for accountability and community
>
> No guru posture. No "10 strategies that print alpha." Just a real person figuring this out.
>
> New recorded videos every 2–3 weeks. Livestreams monthly.
>
> Subscribe if you want to watch the whole thing unfold.

## COIN Framework — Audience Language Document

> COIN = Audience Language + My Language → New Language (signature phrases + tone rules)

### Audience Language (What They Say)
Terms, phrases, and frustrations heard from the ICP (SWE/ML engineers considering quant):

| Category | Their Words |
|----------|------------|
| **How they describe the problem** | "I don't know where to start", "Every guide says something different", "I can code but I don't understand the math", "I feel like I'm studying in isolation" |
| **What they call themselves** | "I'm not a quant, I'm an engineer", "I'm just a coder", "I'm a noob at this", "I don't have a finance background" |
| **How they describe the goal** | "Break into quant finance", "Land a quant dev role", "Build real quant projects", "Actually understand what quants do" |
| **Frustration language** | "Too academic", "Not enough code", "Can't afford EPAT", "Don't know if CPF is worth it", "Wasted money on a course", "Everything is paywalled" |
| **Community signals** | r/quant, r/algotrading, QuantInsti forums, LinkedIn quant posts, "following the channel" |

### My Language (What Vasanth Naturally Says)
Creator voice markers from real communication patterns:

| Category | Vasanth's Words |
|----------|----------------|
| **Honesty signals** | "I'll be real with you", "Honestly", "Here's what actually happened", "Not gonna sugarcoat this" |
| **Learning framing** | "I'm figuring this out too", "This is what clicked for me", "I was confused about this until..." |
| **Identity markers** | "Software engineer", "ML background", "learning in public", "building in public" |
| **Cultural / personal** | Tamil-influenced phrasing, anime/Mahabharata references (organic, not forced), "da" occasionally (community warmth) |
| **Anti-guru stance** | "I'm not an expert", "This isn't financial advice", "I'm still learning this myself" |

### New Language (Signature Phrases + Tone Rules)

| Element | Value |
|---------|-------|
| **Channel signature opener** | "I'm Vasanth. I'm a software engineer learning quant finance in public. Let's figure this out." |
| **Episode closer** | "That's where I am on this. Let me know what clicked and what didn't — I'm reading every comment." |
| **Admission pattern** | "I'll be honest — when I first saw [concept], I had no idea what it meant. Here's how it clicked." |
| **Community invitation** | "If you're studying this too, drop a comment. I want to know who else is on this path." |
| **Anti-hype stamp** | "No alpha promised. No guaranteed returns. Just the honest path." |
| **Tone rule** | Casual but never careless. Self-aware but never self-deprecating to the point of undermining credibility. |
| **Energy level** | 4–5/10 for concept explainers. 6/10 for build videos. 7/10 for milestone/tinder videos. Never fake enthusiasm. |
| **Jargon rule** | Define every technical term the first time it appears, in plain language. No assumptions about prior finance knowledge. |

### COIN Verdict
The New Language for this channel lives at the intersection of: **"The honest student"** + **"The technical peer"** + **"The person who shows the real process"**. Not a teacher. Not a guru. A fellow learner who happens to be a few steps ahead on this particular path.
