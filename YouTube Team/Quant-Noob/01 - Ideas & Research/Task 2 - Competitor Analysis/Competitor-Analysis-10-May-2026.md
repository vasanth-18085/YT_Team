> **Date:** 2026-05-10
> **Channels analyzed:** 8
> **Focus:** Format, title formulas, thumbnail formulas, content gaps, audience overlap

---

## LANDSCAPE SUMMARY

The "quant finance learning" YouTube space in 2026 is:
- **Fragmented** — content is scattered between Python tutorials, finance theory lectures, and career advice. Nobody integrates all three coherently.
- **Guru-heavy** — most channels talk FROM authority, not FROM the learning journey
- **India-side undersupported** — Indian ML/SWE engineers wanting to enter quant have almost no channel built specifically for their situation (working full-time, no finance degree, self-studying)
- **Build-in-public almost nonexistent** — a handful of algo trading code channels exist but don't document the LEARNING journey or the career path

**The gap The Quant Noob fills:** A genuine peer-learning channel that integrates quant finance education + project building + honest career documentation — for an ML/SWE-background audience — in casual English — in public.

## CHANNEL-BY-CHANNEL ANALYSIS

---

### 1. @QuantProgram — The Closest Competitor (~45K subs)

**Channel type:** Quant finance tutorials, Python implementation
**Upload frequency:** 2–3/month
**Avg video length:** 20–40 min
**Avg views/video:** ~8K

#### Title Formula
| Pattern | Example |
|---------|---------|
| "[Concept] in Python — Full Tutorial" | "GARCH in Python — Full Tutorial" |
| "[Strategy] — Complete Implementation" | "Pairs Trading — Complete Python Implementation" |
| "How to [Quant Task] in Python" | "How to Backtest in Python — A Complete Guide" |

**What works:** Python implementation focus pulls high-intent viewers (people who want to code, not just understand).
**What's missing:** Zero personal voice. Zero learning journey. No career context. Videos feel like documentation, not conversation.

#### Thumbnail Formula
- Dark background (navy/black)
- Code snippet or chart visible
- Bold white text, 3–5 words
- No face (screencast only)

**Gap:** No face = no personal connection. The Quant Noob's face-cam approach creates parasocial relationship that QuantProgram will never have.

#### Content they DON'T cover
- Learning journey / personal experience
- How to study quant finance (the meta-question)
- Career path advice for outsiders
- Credential reviews (EPAT, CPF, MScFE)
- Failures and mistakes
- Non-strategy concepts (what is a quant, what does a quant developer do)

### 2. @PatrickBoyle — The Professional Reference (~330K subs)

**Channel type:** Professional quant finance — hedge fund context, sophisticated
**Upload frequency:** 1–2/month
**Avg video length:** 25–60 min
**Avg views/video:** ~80K

#### What he does well
- Authoritative. He ran a hedge fund. The credibility is real.
- Long-form, deep content that his audience appreciates
- Very British, dry wit — works for his audience

#### Why he's NOT competition for The Quant Noob
- He talks TO beginners from above. We talk WITH them as peers.
- His audience: finance professionals, MBA graduates, CFA holders
- Our audience: ML engineers, CS grads, self-taught programmers
- No build-in-public, no Python code, no learning journey

**The opportunity:** Patrick Boyle's viewers who want to actually implement what he talks about will find The Quant Noob. **Design V17 (Triple Barrier Method) and V15 (GARCH) to target "Patrick Boyle viewers who want to code it."**

## THUMBNAIL FORMULA (From Competitor Analysis)

What works in this niche (face-cam + finance/code):

| Element | Spec |
|---------|------|
| **Face** | Real expression matching the video emotion — not a posed smile. Surprise, confusion, satisfaction. |
| **Background** | Dark (matches code environment) or blurred desk setup. Clean. |
| **Text overlay** | 3–5 words. Bold. High contrast. Often a question or a punch. |
| **Text color** | White or electric blue. Never yellow (too clickbait). |
| **Secondary element** | A chart, code snippet, or book cover relevant to the topic — max 1 additional element. |
| **Emotion principle** | Thumbnail should match the video's honest emotion, not oversell it. "Confused but curious" > fake excitement. |
