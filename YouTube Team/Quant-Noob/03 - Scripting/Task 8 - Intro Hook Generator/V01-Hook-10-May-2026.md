## HOOK TYPE EXPLORATION (at least 3 types)

### Hook Type 1 — Shocking Statement

> "Most quant finance YouTube is made by people who already know quant finance.
> 
> This channel is different. I don't know quant finance yet.
> 
> And I'm going to learn it in public — right here — so you can watch exactly what it takes."

**R.A.I.N.Y Check:**
- R ✅ — "watch exactly what it takes" (you get to see the real learning process)
- A ✅ — "I don't know it yet" (addresses "fake expert" objection pre-emptively)
- I ✅ — under 40 seconds
- W ✅ — implied: most channels are post-expertise. This one is in-expertise.
- Y ✅ — "I'm doing this right now, in real time"

## FINAL HOOK SELECTION

**Primary Hook: Type 3 (Storytelling) — Revised**

> "A few months ago I made a plan.
> 
> I called it the Quant Dharma Plan. Three years to go from ML engineer — which is what I am — to working in quant finance.
> 
> I don't know yet if it'll work. But I'm doing it on YouTube so you can watch every step — the parts that click, the parts that don't, and everything I figure out along the way.
> 
> I'm Vasanth. Let's get into it."

**Why this hook:**
- The name "Quant Dharma Plan" is specific and memorable — not generic
- "I don't know yet if it'll work" is the single most differentiating line a new channel can open with
- "ML engineer — which is what I am" establishes authority and ICP in one phrase
- 4 short paragraphs, all under 40 seconds combined
- Tone: honest, direct, not trying too hard

**R.A.I.N.Y Final Check:**
| R.A.I.N.Y | Met? | How |
|-----------|------|-----|
| Result | ✅ | "watch every step — the parts that click, the parts that don't" |
| Address Objections | ✅ | "I don't know yet if it'll work" — disarms guru skepticism immediately |
| Instant | ✅ | ~25 seconds total |
| Why Now | ✅ | "I'm doing it" — present tense, ongoing journey |
| Why You | ✅ | "ML engineer — which is what I am" + specific named plan |

## HOOK NOTES FOR FILMING

- Deliver the hook WITHOUT reading off a script. It should feel like you're telling a friend.
- The key phrase to get right: "I don't know yet if it'll work." Say it with calm honesty, not anxiety or false modesty.
- "Let's get into it." — should feel like an invitation, not a transition line. Lean in slightly.
- Film 3 takes minimum. Use the one that feels most natural, not the one that's most "performed."
