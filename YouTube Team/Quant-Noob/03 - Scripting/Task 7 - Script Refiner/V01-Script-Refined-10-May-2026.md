### [INTRO — After Hook, ~30 seconds in]

"My name is Vasanth. I'm a software engineer with an ML background.

And I'm teaching myself quant finance — while working full time — in public.

This channel is where I document the whole thing."

---

### [THE GAP — Why this channel exists]

"When I started looking into quant finance, I hit a wall.

The advanced tutorials assumed I already knew the field. The finance channels didn't touch code. The Python tutorials didn't explain why you'd use any of it in markets.

There was no channel for someone in my position — a software engineer who's curious about quant finance but starting from zero.

So I made one."

---

### [THE PLAN — 3 years, honest breakdown]

"I made what I call the Quant Dharma Plan. It's a 3-year learning roadmap.

Year 1: I'm doing the CPF — the Certificate in Python and Finance from QuantInsti. I'm also doing EPAT, which is their more advanced quant trading program. And I'm building my math foundations — linear algebra, probability, stochastic processes.

CPF and EPAT are real credentials that quant firms recognize. They're not shortcuts — they're structured paths through the material.

On top of that, I'll build two projects this year. The first one is a market data engine — basically a system that pulls, cleans, and stores financial data. Nothing glamorous, but a real foundation.

Year 2: I'm planning to do the MScFE — the Master's in Financial Engineering from World Quant University. It's fully online and free. It goes deep — derivatives pricing, portfolio optimization, econometrics.

And I'll keep building more sophisticated projects. A backtesting framework. A risk model. Real systems, documented publicly.

Year 3: This is where I want to be contributing — open source tools, maybe research papers. And working in a quant role. Quant developer, quant researcher — somewhere the ML and finance worlds actually meet.

That's the plan. Three years. Documented here."

---

### [WHO THIS IS FOR]

"If you're an ML or software engineer who keeps looking at quant finance and thinking 'that's where I want to be' — this channel is for you.

If you're working full-time and studying something hard in the hours you can find — this channel is for you.

If you want to watch someone figure it out in real time, not just teach from a position of already knowing it — this channel is definitely for you."

---

### [WHAT THIS CHANNEL IS NOT]

"Quick note on what you won't get here.

I'm not going to tell you about trading strategies that print money. I don't have those.

I'm not going to pretend I understand something when I don't. If I'm lost, I'll tell you.

This is a learning channel. Not a guru channel. The difference matters."

---

### [WHAT TO EXPECT]

"Here's what videos on this channel look like.

Concept explainers — I'll take hard quant finance concepts and explain them the way an engineer would. With intuition first. Math second. Code when it matters.

Build-in-public videos — I'll document the projects I'm building. Real code. Real decisions. What went wrong and why.

Milestone updates — when I pass exams, finish a project, or hit a point where I have something real to report. You'll see the full picture.

And livestreams — study-with-me and build sessions where you can watch in real time."

---

### [THE NAME]

"One thing about the channel name — The Quant Noob.

That's not ironic. That's accurate.

I am a noob in this field. Right now. And I think that's the most honest thing I can put in a channel name.

The interesting part isn't being a noob. The interesting part is documenting the journey from noob to something more — so the next person who's in the same position has a reference point."

---

### [HONEST UNCERTAINTY]

"I'll tell you what I don't know.

I don't know if this 3-year plan will work exactly as written. Probably it won't. Plans change when reality hits.

I don't know how hard the math will get. I can do linear algebra. I've done probability from my ML work. But stochastic calculus — which I'll hit in Year 2 — is territory I haven't entered yet.

What I know is that the worst outcome of this plan is: I become a better engineer and I understand financial markets far better than I do today.

That's not a bad outcome."

---

### [CTA — Task 10 will finalize wording, this is placeholder]

"If this is something you want to follow, subscribe. This is a multi-year commitment on my end. I'll keep showing up.

And if you're on a similar path — learning quant finance, making a career transition, trying to get into a world that feels hard to enter — tell me where you are in the comments. I genuinely want to know.

Let's figure this out."
