## BRAIN DUMP — EVERYTHING I WANT TO SAY

*(Written as if talking freely — no editing, no polish, this is the raw material)*

---

**Why I'm making this channel:**

I'm a software engineer. I've been doing ML work — building models, data pipelines, working with teams. But I kept looking at quant finance and thinking... that's where the hard problems are. That's where math + code + real-world stakes actually meet. And I wasn't in that world. I had no idea how to get there.

So I made a plan. I called it the Quant Dharma Plan. It's a 3-year plan to go from where I am now — ML engineer who doesn't really know quant finance — to someone who can actually work in this field. Not just learn it for fun. Actually get there.

And I thought — why not document it? Because I looked online and I couldn't find what I was looking for. I found channels with advanced quant tutorials that assume you already know everything. I found finance channels that don't touch code. I found Python tutorials that don't explain why you're doing what you're doing in a finance context. I didn't find someone just... doing the journey and showing you what it looks like.

So that's what this channel is. The real version. The uncomfortable version. Where I'll show you when something clicks and when I'm completely lost.

---

**What the plan actually is:**

Year 1 — I'm doing CPF (Certificate in Python and Finance from QuantInsti). I'm already doing EPAT actually. And I'm studying the foundational math — linear algebra, probability theory, stochastic processes. I'm also going to start building projects. The first project is a market data engine — just getting clean financial data flowing through a system I built.

Year 2 — This is where it gets deeper. I'm planning to do the MScFE from World Quant University. That's their master's in Financial Engineering — free, fully online, and actually respected in the industry. The coursework gets into derivatives pricing, portfolio optimization, econometrics. And I'll keep building more sophisticated projects.

Year 3 — This is where I want to be contributing to the field. Open source quant tools. Research papers if I get there. And by this point I want to be working in a quant role — quant developer, quant researcher, something where the ML and finance worlds meet.

---

**What this channel is NOT:**

I'm not going to pretend I have it figured out. I'm not going to post "10 alpha strategies that work" because I don't have 10 alpha strategies that work. I'm not a hedge fund manager. I haven't made millions in the markets. I'm a software engineer who's learning this stuff and being honest about it.

If you want a guru who tells you the secrets to trading success — wrong channel. If you want someone actually doing the work and showing you every step — this might be the channel for you.

---

**Who this is for:**

If you're an ML engineer or a software engineer who's curious about quant finance but doesn't know where to start — this is for you.

If you're already in a technical role and you're wondering if the quant world is accessible to you without a finance degree — this is for you.

If you're learning quant finance on your own, in your spare time, after work — this is absolutely for you. Because that's exactly what I'm doing.

---

**What to expect from this channel:**

Concept explainers — I'll explain the math and the models in a way that makes sense if you have an ML or engineering background.

Build-in-public videos — I'll document the actual projects I'm building. The code, the decisions, what went wrong.

Honest updates — milestone videos when I pass exams, progress check-ins, year reviews.

Live sessions — I'll do study-with-me and build-in-public livestreams. You can watch me work in real time.

---

**A specific thing I want to say:**

The reason I'm calling this The Quant Noob is intentional. I am a noob. Right now. And I'm fine with that. The thing that makes this interesting is the journey from noob to not-noob. That's what I'm documenting.

I remember when I started in ML — I felt completely lost. Stack traces everywhere. Papers I couldn't understand. And then things started clicking, one by one. I think quant finance is going to be the same process. And I want to show that process.

---

**What I want you to do:**

Subscribe if this sounds like something you want to follow. Because this is a multi-year journey. If you want to watch the whole thing — the wins, the failures, the confusion, the clarity — subscribe and I'll keep showing up.

And if you're also doing something like this — learning quant finance, making a career transition, trying to get into a world that feels hard to enter — I want to hear from you. Comment below. Tell me where you are in the journey.

---

**Things I'm nervous to say (but should):**

I don't know if this plan will work. I genuinely don't know if in 3 years I'll be working as a quant. But I think the worst that happens is I become a much better engineer and know a lot more about how markets work. That's not a bad outcome.

I also don't know how hard the math is going to get. I have a decent foundation — I did linear algebra in college, I know probability from my ML work. But stochastic calculus, for example, is something I've never touched. I'm going to try and explain it when I get there, but I might get lost. And that's... kind of the point of this channel.

---

**Final thing — the name:**

The Quant Noob. Not "Quant Finance Hub" or "Quant Academy" or anything that sounds like a professional channel. The Noob part is intentional. It's what I am. It's what most people in this space are when they start. And I think there's something powerful about owning that.
