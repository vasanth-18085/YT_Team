### Reddit Account — Special Notes

Reddit is hostile to self-promotion from new accounts. You MUST:

1. **Create account 2-4 weeks before first video** — aged accounts get less suspicion
2. **DO NOT use your channel name as username** — it screams self-promoter
3. **Build karma first** — comment helpfully on 15-20 posts in quant/ML subreddits before EVER sharing your own content
4. **Read each subreddit's rules** — many ban direct YouTube links. You'll need to share value first, link second.
5. **Never say "check out my video"** — frame it as "I wrote up/recorded an explanation of X, here's the breakdown" and put the link casually

### Twitter/X Account — Special Notes

1. **Bio matters** — something like: "ML engineer exploring quant finance. Building in public. Videos on quantitative trading concepts."
2. **Profile pic** — your face or a clean logo. Not default egg.
3. **Follow 50-100 quant/ML accounts immediately** — this trains your feed and makes you visible
4. **Start tweeting 1-2 weeks before first video** — even just thoughts, questions, or sharing others' work with your take on it

## 2. Pre-Upload Strategy

Everything that happens BEFORE you click "Publish" on YouTube.

### 2.1 — Video Metadata Preparation (Do 1 day before upload)

| Element | What to Prepare | Tips |
|---------|-----------------|------|
| **Title** | Final title (under 60 chars) | Use US/global terms. "S&P 500" > "NIFTY" for US reach. No jargon in title unless it's what people search. |
| **Description** | First 2 lines = hook + keyword-rich summary. Rest = timestamps, links, resources. | First 150 chars show in search results. Make them count. |
| **Tags** | 8-12 tags mixing broad + specific | e.g., "algorithmic trading", "machine learning trading", "quant finance", "meta labeling", "python trading" |
| **Thumbnail** | Designed and exported (1280x720, <2MB) | High contrast, 3 words max text, your face if comfortable |
| **End screen** | Plan which video to link to | Subscribe button + next video + playlist |
| **Cards** | 1-2 cards pointing to related videos | Place at natural transition points |
| **Subtitles/CC** | Auto-generated is fine to start. Review for accuracy. | Subtitles help SEO and accessibility |
| **Chapters/Timestamps** | Write timestamps in description | Format: `0:00 Intro \| 1:30 What is X \| 4:20 Implementation` |

### 2.2 — Community Seeding Posts (Prepare 1-2 days before)

Write these posts BEFORE upload day so you're not scrambling:

**Reddit post draft:**
- Write a genuine, value-first post about the TOPIC (not the video)
- The video link goes at the end as "I also recorded a deeper walkthrough here if anyone's interested"
- Or: make a text post summarizing the concept with the video as supplementary

**Twitter/X thread draft:**
- 4-6 tweet thread breaking down the core concept of your video
- Last tweet: "Full walkthrough with code + visuals: [YouTube link]"
- First tweet must hook — start with a surprising fact or contrarian take

**LinkedIn post draft:**
- More professional framing
- "Here's what I learned about [X] while building [Y]..."
- Link in comments (LinkedIn suppresses posts with links in body)

### 2.3 — Upload Scheduling Decision

| Your Target Audience | Best Upload Time (IST) | Why |
|---------------------|------------------------|-----|
| US East Coast (primary for quant) | **5:30 - 6:30 AM IST** | = 8-9 PM EST previous night. US viewers see it fresh in morning feed. |
| US West Coast | **8:00 AM IST** | = 7:30 PM PST. Evening viewers. |
| UK/Europe | **1:30 - 2:30 PM IST** | = 9-10 AM GMT. Morning coffee crowd. |
| India | **6:00 - 7:00 PM IST** | Evening after work. |
| **Recommended default** | **6:00 AM IST** | Catches US evening + shows up in US morning feeds. Your niche is US-heavy. |

**Schedule uploads in YouTube Studio** — don't manually publish. Schedule 1 day before so YouTube can process the video fully (HD, subtitles, etc).

### 2.4 — Pre-Upload Checklist

```
□ Video uploaded as unlisted/scheduled (not public yet)
□ Title finalized — under 60 chars, keyword-rich
□ Description written — timestamps, links, first 2 lines optimized
□ Thumbnail uploaded — high contrast, readable on mobile
□ Tags added (8-12)
□ End screen configured (subscribe + next video)
□ Cards added at transition points
□ Category set to "Education" or "Science & Technology"
□ Language set to English
□ Playlist assigned
□ Reddit post drafted
□ Twitter thread drafted
□ LinkedIn post drafted
□ Community tab post drafted (once you have 500+ subs)
□ Publish time scheduled for 6:00 AM IST
```

## 3. Upload Day Execution

### Hour 0 — Video Goes Live (6:00 AM IST)

1. **Verify it's public** — check from an incognito window
2. **Watch the first 30 seconds yourself** — make sure no processing issues
3. **Pin a comment** on your own video — ask a question or add bonus context
   - Example: "What's one concept from this video you want me to go deeper on? Drop it below 👇"
4. **Share to your own social** immediately (Twitter, LinkedIn)

### First 2 Hours (6-8 AM IST) — The Critical Seed Window

This is where you engineer your initial audience. The algorithm watches WHO engages first and recommends to similar viewers.

**Priority order:**

1. **Twitter/X** — Post your thread immediately. Tag relevant accounts (not spam-tag, genuine tags of people whose work you referenced)
2. **Reddit** — Post in 1-2 relevant subreddits (see Community Map below). Follow each sub's rules exactly.
3. **LinkedIn** — Post with link in first comment
4. **Discord servers** — Share in appropriate channels of servers you're already active in (NOT servers you just joined today)

### Hours 2-12 — Engage With Every Response

- Reply to EVERY comment on YouTube within the first 12 hours
- Reply to every Reddit comment on your post
- Reply to every Twitter reply
- The algorithm tracks creator engagement — it boosts videos where the creator is active in comments

### Hour 12-24 — Second Push

- If the Reddit post did well, it's running on its own now — don't touch it
- If it didn't gain traction, DON'T delete and repost (Reddit flags this). Let it be.
- Post a follow-up tweet — a single insight or "one thing I didn't include in the video"
- Check YouTube Analytics: look at CTR and Average View Duration

## 4. Post-Upload Strategy

### First 48 Hours — The Algorithm Decision Window

YouTube decides in the first 48 hours whether to push your video wider. What matters:

| Signal | What You Control | Target |
|--------|-----------------|--------|
| **CTR (Click-Through Rate)** | Thumbnail + Title | >5% from browse, >10% from search |
| **Average View Duration** | Content quality (already locked in) | >50% of video length |
| **Engagement rate** | Pin comment, ask questions, reply | As many comments as possible early |
| **External traffic** | Reddit, Twitter, LinkedIn shares | At least SOME non-YouTube traffic in first 24h |

### Days 2-7 — Sustained Momentum

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| **Day 2** | Reply to any new comments. Share a "behind the scenes" or learning tweet. |
| **Day 3** | If video is performing well (>500 views), share to one MORE community you haven't hit yet. |
| **Day 4** | Tweet a follow-up insight related to the video topic. Don't link the video — just provide value. People will find your profile → channel. |
| **Day 5** | Check Analytics. Note what's working (which traffic source is biggest?). Write down learnings for next video. |
| **Day 6-7** | Start teasing next video topic. "Working on something about [X] next..." |

### Week 2-4 — Long Tail

- The video will either plateau or get picked up by search/suggested. You can't force this.
- What you CAN do: keep your channel active. The algorithm favors channels that upload consistently.
- Engage with comments that keep trickling in.
- If a related trending topic appears (news, paper release, market event), tweet about it and link back to your video as "relevant context."

## 5. Ongoing Community Building

### Weekly Habits (30-45 min/day total)

| Platform | Daily Time | What to Do |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| **Reddit** | 10 min | Comment on 2-3 posts in quant/ML subs. Genuine, helpful comments. No self-promotion unless directly relevant. |
| **Twitter/X** | 15 min | 1 original tweet (thought, insight, question). Reply to 3-5 others in your niche. Retweet 1-2 interesting things with your take. |
| **LinkedIn** | 5 min | 2-3 times per week. Comment on others' posts. Share 1 post per week (not always your videos). |
| **YouTube comments** | 10 min | Reply to all new comments on your videos. Comment on 2-3 other creators' videos in your niche (genuine comments, not "great video check mine out"). |
| **Discord** | 5 min | Hang out, answer questions if you can. Be a member, not a billboard. |

### The 80/20 Rule of Self-Promotion

**80% giving value** (comments, insights, helping others, sharing knowledge) →
**20% sharing your own stuff** (video links, channel mentions)

If you invert this ratio, every platform will punish you — shadow bans, downvotes, unfollows, or outright bans.

## 6. Platform-Specific Playbooks

### Reddit — The Most Important Platform for US Seed Audience

**How Reddit works (for a beginner):**
- Reddit is organized into "subreddits" (communities) — like r/algotrading
- Each sub has its own rules, culture, and moderators
- Users upvote/downvote content. High upvotes = visibility.
- "Karma" = your reputation score (sum of upvotes on your posts/comments)
- New accounts with low karma are automatically filtered in most subs

**Your Reddit strategy:**

| Phase | Timeline | Actions |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Setup** | Week 1-2 | Create account. Subscribe to all subs listed in Community Map. |
| **Lurk** | Week 2-3 | Read 10-15 posts daily. Understand the culture. Note what gets upvoted. Note what gets removed. |
| **Comment** | Week 3-5 | Start commenting helpfully. Answer questions. Share insights. Build karma to 100+. |
| **First post** | Week 5+ (after first video) | Share your video in ONE sub. Value-first framing. |
| **Ongoing** | Forever | 80% commenting/helping, 20% sharing your content. Max 1 self-promotional post per sub per week. |

**Dos:**
- Write long, thoughtful answers to questions (Reddit rewards effort)
- Use the post's context to frame your video link naturally
- Cross-post between subs ONLY if the content fits both
- Use text posts with video link inside (not direct video link posts — those get auto-removed in many subs)

**Don'ts:**
- Never post the same link to 5 subs simultaneously (looks like spam, gets flagged)
- Never say "subscribe to my channel" on Reddit (instant downvotes)
- Never argue with trolls or negative comments (just don't reply)
- Never delete a post that's getting downvoted (Reddit tracks this, looks suspicious)
- Never use multiple accounts to upvote yourself (bannable offense, Reddit detects this)

### Twitter/X — Where Quant/ML People Actually Hang Out

**How Twitter/X works (for a beginner):**
- 280 character tweets. Threads = multiple tweets chained together.
- "Retweet" = share someone's tweet to your followers
- "Quote Tweet" = share + add your own comment
- Hashtags help discovery but don't overuse (1-2 per tweet max)
- Followers grow through consistency + engaging with others

**Your Twitter strategy:**

| Phase | Timeline | Actions |
|-------|----------|---------|
| **Setup** | Day 1 | Bio, profile pic, pin a tweet about what you're building. Follow 50-100 quant/ML accounts. |
| **Learn the culture** | Week 1 | See what quant Twitter talks about. What gets engagement. What's cringe. |
| **Start tweeting** | Week 1-2 | 1 tweet/day minimum. Thoughts on what you're learning, questions, takes on papers/news. |
| **Engage** | Daily | Reply to 5+ tweets from bigger accounts. Be insightful, not sycophantic. |
| **First video promo** | When video drops | Thread format. Value first, link last. |

**What to tweet (when you have nothing to promote):**
- "TIL [interesting quant concept]" — share what you learned today
- "Unpopular opinion: [take on common quant practice]" — spark discussion
- Screenshots of your code/charts with brief explanation
- Reshare a paper/article with your 1-2 sentence take
- Ask genuine questions — "How do people handle [X] in live trading?"
- "Thread: [concept] explained simply" — educational threads grow followers fast

**Accounts to follow and engage with (starting list):**
- @QuantProgrammer, @ErnestPChan, @QuantInsti, @Auquan
- @kaboroflow, @Marcos_Lopez_de (if active)
- @PythonQuants, @QuantMag
- Search for: "quant trading", "algo trading", "systematic trading" in bios
- Follow whoever THEY follow — this builds your network map

**Thread format for video promotion:**

```
Tweet 1: Hook — surprising fact or contrarian take about the topic
Tweet 2: "Here's what most people get wrong about [X]..."
Tweet 3: The core insight from your video
Tweet 4: A chart/visual from your video (images get 2x engagement)
Tweet 5: "Full breakdown with code walkthrough: [YouTube link]"
Tweet 6: "What's your take on [question related to topic]?" (drives replies)
```

### LinkedIn — Professional Credibility + Indian Audience

**Your LinkedIn strategy:**

- Post 2-3 times per week (not all video promos)
- Frame everything professionally: "Here's what I learned building X"
- PUT LINKS IN THE FIRST COMMENT, not the post body (LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links)
- Use your real identity — LinkedIn is where professional trust lives
- Connect with QuantInsti people, EPAT alumni, ML engineers

**Post types that work on LinkedIn:**
- "I spent 3 hours debugging [X]. Here's what I learned:" (relatable struggle posts)
- "Concept breakdown with a clean visual" (educational carousel/image posts)
- "Hot take on industry practice" (engagement bait but genuine)
- Video announcement (but focus on what THEY'll learn, not "I made a video")

## 9. Metrics to Track

### YouTube Analytics — Check Weekly

| Metric | What It Tells You | Healthy Range (early channel) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| **CTR** | Is your title/thumbnail working? | 4-10% (higher for search, lower for browse) |
| **Average View Duration** | Is your content holding attention? | >50% of video length |
| **Traffic Sources** | Where are viewers finding you? | Diverse sources = healthy |
| **Geography** | Which countries watch you? | Track US/UK/India split |
| **Subscriber conversion** | Are viewers subscribing? | 2-5% of viewers |
| **Impressions** | Is YouTube showing your video? | Should grow week over week |

### Social Media Metrics — Check Monthly

| Platform | Track | Healthy Growth (first 6 months) |
|----------|-------|----------------------------------|
| **Twitter** | Followers, avg likes per tweet, replies | 5-20 new followers/week with consistent posting |
| **Reddit** | Karma, upvotes on posts, comment engagement | Karma >500 in first 2 months |
| **LinkedIn** | Connections, post impressions | 50-100 new connections/month |

### What to Do With Metrics

- **High CTR + Low AVD** = Thumbnail/title overpromises. Content doesn't deliver. Fix content pacing.
- **Low CTR + High AVD** = Content is great but nobody's clicking. Fix thumbnail/title.
- **Lots of search traffic** = Your SEO is working. Double down on searchable titles.
- **Lots of suggested traffic** = Algorithm is recommending you. You're winning.
- **Reddit driving views but low AVD from Reddit** = Reddit audience clicks but doesn't stay. Maybe your video is too long for casual browsers — add timestamps.
- **Twitter engagement high but no YouTube clicks** = Your threads are TOO complete. Hold back more value for the video.

## 10. Common Mistakes to Avoid

### The Beginner Traps

| Mistake | Why It's Tempting | Why It Hurts |
|---------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Spamming links everywhere on day 1** | You're excited about your video | Every platform will shadow-ban or downvote you. Trust = 0. |
| **Creating accounts only to promote** | "I just need to share the link" | Platforms detect promo-only accounts. Reddit bans them. |
| **Posting the same text on every platform** | Saves time | Each platform has different culture. Copy-paste looks lazy and inauthentic. |
| **Ignoring comments/replies** | You're busy making the next video | Early engagement signals matter enormously. Reply to everything in first 48h. |
| **Deleting posts that don't perform** | Embarrassment | Platforms track this. Looks suspicious. Leave it. Learn from it. |
| **Changing upload schedule constantly** | "Maybe Thursday is better" | Algorithm rewards consistency. Pick a day. Stick to it for 3+ months. |
| **Obsessing over analytics daily** | Dopamine/anxiety | Check weekly max. Daily checking changes nothing and drains energy. |
| **Comparing to established channels** | "They get 100k views, I get 50" | They've been at it for years. Your month 1 ≠ their month 60. |
| **Buying subscribers/views** | Quick numbers feel good | YouTube detects this. Kills your channel's algorithmic trust permanently. |
| **Making content for the algorithm instead of your audience** | "Trending topics get views" | If it's not in your niche, the viewers won't subscribe or come back. |

### The Self-Promotion Balance

**Rule of thumb for Reddit:**
- First 30 days: ZERO self-promotion. Only comment and engage.
- After that: Max 1 self-promotional post per subreddit per 7 days
- Your post history should be 90%+ non-promotional

**Rule of thumb for Twitter:**
- 1 promotional tweet for every 10 non-promotional tweets
- Promotional = contains your YouTube link
- Non-promotional = thoughts, replies, retweets, questions, insights

**Rule of thumb for LinkedIn:**
- 1 video post for every 3-4 regular posts
- Regular posts = insights, career thoughts, industry commentary

## Final Note — The Long Game

Month 1-3: **Crickets.** This is normal. You're building in public to an empty room. Your job is to be consistent and build community presence.

Month 3-6: **Slow traction.** If you've been genuine in communities, people start recognizing your name. Videos get 100-500 views.

Month 6-12: **Compounding.** Back catalog starts getting search traffic. Algorithm has enough data on your channel to recommend you. Community knows you.

**The creators who win are the ones who kept uploading when nobody was watching.** Every video you make with 50 views is still practice, still SEO, still building your catalog for when the algorithm does pick you up.

Your niche (quant ML, systematic trading, financial machine learning) is small but HIGH VALUE. You don't need 100k subscribers to build a meaningful audience and revenue. 5,000 dedicated quant/ML viewers is worth more than 100,000 casual viewers — in CPM, in course sales, in career opportunities, in everything.

Play the long game. Be genuine. Provide value. The numbers follow.
