## Revenue Philosophy

MLQuant revenue is the gravy, not the meal. The Dharma plan is funded by Zoho salary. YouTube income augments it — it doesn't replace it. This means we can afford to hold the line on "free always" without compromise.

**Target by scenario (from Dharma plan):**
- Conservative (50% likely): ₹1K/month by 2028, ₹4K/month by 2029
- Realistic (35% likely): ₹5K/month by 2028, ₹15K/month by 2029
- Optimistic (15% likely): ₹11K/month by 2028, ₹28K/month by 2029

This is a niche English-language channel. CPM will be $3–8 (₹250–660) per 1,000 views. It won't be significant revenue at sub-10K subs. The financial upside is real — it just takes time.

**When to launch:** After 500 subscribers. Don't launch earlier — no social proof.

## Revenue Stream 4 — Gumroad Practice Guide (Year 2, Post 1K subs)

A hands-on implementation guide — NOT the main content (that stays free forever).

**Product:** "The MLQuant Practice Guide" — 7 project workbooks with:
- Skeleton code (structure given, logic holes for the learner to fill)
- Test cases to verify each implementation
- Dataset links (NSE data, preprocessed for the projects)
- Extended exercises beyond the YouTube videos

**Price:** ₹1,499 (~$18). Not a course. Not a paywall. Practice material for those who want guided self-testing.

**Launch criteria:** Only after at least P1–P3 are complete on YouTube. Don't sell a practice guide for content that doesn't exist yet.

**Honest projection:** 5–10 sales/month at ₹1,499 = ₹7,500–15,000/month at scale.

**Dharma check:** The content is free. This is the worksheet. Like a textbook whose chapter explanations are free on YouTube but the problem set book costs money. That's fine.

## Task 18 — Creator Collabs (Post-Launch)

**Strategy:** Don't pursue collabs in Year 1. Build the channel's identity first.

**Year 2 target collaborations:**
- Quant/finance educators in the Indian ML space (5K–50K subs, aligned values)
- Open-source quant tool maintainers (QuantLib, Zipline, VectorBT)
- MScFE peers who also create content

**Approach:** Collaboration should be content-driven, not view-driven. "We're both building factor models for Indian markets" is a collab. "You have an audience, I have an audience, let's cross-promote" is not.

**Format:** Guest appearance in a project video ("here's how [collaborator] approached the same problem"), not swap interviews.
