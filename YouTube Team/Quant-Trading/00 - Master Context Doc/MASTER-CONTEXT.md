## 7. Myths & Bad Common Advice

1. **"Just use LSTM for stock prediction"** — The #1 beginner trap. No walk-forward validation, no realistic assumptions, teaches nothing about actual quant trading.
2. **"This alpha/strategy is guaranteed to work"** — Selling signals or "best working alphas" without any statistical rigor or out-of-sample testing.
3. **"You need a PhD in math to do quant"** — Gatekeeping myth. ML skills + structured learning can get you very far without a PhD.
4. **"Backtesting = running your model on historical data"** — Nobody shows real-world backtesting with proper walk-forward validation, transaction costs, slippage, etc.
5. **"If you can't afford paid courses, you can't learn this"** — The good stuff is hoarded behind paywalls, but it doesn't have to be.
6. **"More indicators = better strategy"** — Copy-pasting 20 indicators from TradingView doesn't make a quant strategy.
