## 1. Channel Name — Recommendations

Your channel name needs to do ONE thing: make a stranger immediately understand what this channel is about. In your niche (ML + Quant Finance), clarity beats cleverness.

### Criteria
- **Instantly understandable** — no decoding required
- **No numbers, underscores, or random characters** — looks amateur
- **Memorable at small sizes** — works in YouTube comments, search results, phone screens
- **Niche-signalling** — a CS/ML person should feel "this is for me" just from the name

### Options

| Name | Pros | Cons |
|------|------|------|
| **QuantML** | Ultra clean. Two concepts fused. Easy to say, type, search. Instantly signals "quant + machine learning". | Might feel too narrow if you expand scope later. |
| **Shadow Quant** | Sounds cool, mysterious. Has personality. | "Shadow" doesn't communicate anything about ML or learning. A stranger won't know what this channel is about from the name alone. |
| **Quant Code** | Clear: quant + coding. Signals hands-on. Short. | Slightly generic. Doesn't signal ML specifically. |
| **ML Quant Lab** | Signals ML + Quant + experimentation. Feels like a workspace. | Slightly longer. "Lab" is overused. |
| **AlgoML** | Short. Signals algorithmic/algo trading + ML. | Less clear than QuantML. "Algo" can mean many things. |
| **Quant From Scratch** | Signals the beginner-friendly angle directly. Memorable. | Long. Less punchy as a channel brand. |

### My recommendation: **QuantML**
It's clean, searchable, two syllables, and instantly tells your ideal viewer "this is machine learning applied to quant finance." It also works as a brand — you can have a QuantML GitHub, QuantML community, etc.

**But this is YOUR channel.** Pick what feels right to you. The name matters less than you think — consistency and content matter 10x more. A great channel with an okay name will always beat a dead channel with a perfect name.

> **Decision needed:** Pick a name (or shortlist 2-3 to sit with for a day).

## 2. Face on Camera — Guidance

This is one of the biggest decisions and here's the honest breakdown:

### Face on camera
- **Trust builds faster.** Viewers subscribe to people, not brands. A face in thumbnails gets 2-5x more clicks.
- **Your niche allows it.** Quant content + a real person explaining = credibility. Think 3Blue1Brown started faceless but most successful edu-creators show face eventually.
- **Community is stronger.** People connect with a human. Your "nerdy friend" tone works best with a face.

### Faceless / Anonymous
- **Privacy at work.** If you're worried about your office finding out, faceless is safer.
- **Easier to start.** No camera anxiety, no lighting setup, just screen recordings + voice.
- **Works in your niche.** Screen coding content + voiceover is totally viable for quant/ML.

### My recommendation
**Start faceless, add face later when comfortable.** Here's why:
- You haven't started yet. Getting videos OUT matters more than getting videos perfect.
- Your content type (coding, systems, projects) works great as screen recordings + voiceover.
- You can introduce your face gradually — maybe in intros first, then thumbnails.
- Many successful coding channels started faceless and added face later (Fireship, TechLead started screen-heavy).

This is NOT a permanent decision. It's a "get started" decision.

> **Decision needed:** Faceless to start? Or face from day 1?

## 3. Profile Picture

### If Faceless
Use a **clean, simple logo mark** — not text, not a complex illustration. Something that reads clearly at 40x40 pixels (how it appears in YouTube comments).

Options:
- A simple geometric icon (think: a brain merged with a candlestick chart, or a neural network node pattern)
- Use Canva or Midjourney to generate options
- Color: pick ONE primary color and stick with it (dark blues, teals, or greens signal "tech + finance")

### If Face
- Well-lit headshot, clean background
- Just your head and shoulders — zoomed in enough to read at tiny sizes
- Slight smile or neutral confident expression
- This becomes your brand across all platforms

### Rules Either Way
- **No text in the profile picture** — it's unreadable at comment size
- **No busy backgrounds** — it disappears in YouTube's busy UI
- **High contrast** — should pop on both white and dark mode backgrounds

## 6. Title Direction

Your titles should make your ideal viewer (ML-aware beginner, zero QF) feel like this channel was built specifically for them. Based on the Master Context Doc, effective title patterns for your niche:

### Patterns That Will Work
- **"I Built [specific system] from Scratch"** — shows real output, not theory
- **"[QF Concept] Explained for ML Engineers"** — bridges ML→QF gap directly
- **"Why [Common Approach] Doesn't Work in Real Trading"** — myth-busting, contrarian
- **"From Jupyter Notebook to Live Trading System"** — their exact pain→pleasure journey
- **"The [X] That Actually Works in Quant Finance"** — signals real-world validity

### Patterns to AVOID
- "LSTM Stock Price Prediction Tutorial" — this is the toy project your viewers are trying to escape
- "How to Get Rich with AI Trading" — hype, destroys trust instantly
- "Quant Finance for Beginners Part 1" — boring, no hook, playlist-bait

Title strategy will be refined in Task 4 (Title Generator) with the full 100-framework system.
