## What COIN Does for This Channel

On Vyasa's Witness, COIN serves a specific purpose: **the channel speaks with academic accuracy but not academic gatekeeping.** The viewer is the diaspora adult who grew up with the TV show and wants to be taken seriously — not talked down to, not confused by jargon, not lied to with soft mythology framing.

# PART 2 — My Language (Vyasa's Witness Voice)

*These are the terms, constructions, and positions that define how this channel speaks. These are non-negotiable for consistency.*

## Core Terms

| Term | How We Use It |
|------|--------------|
| BORI Critical Edition | Full name on first use. "BORI text" or "the text" after that. Never "the original" or "the Sanskrit version" — be specific. |
| Bibek Debroy | Named in every video description. "Debroy's translation" — not "the English version." |
| Parva | Always named. "Adi Parva" not "the first book." |
| Atiratha / Maharatha / Ardha-Rathi | Always defined on first use per video. "Half a warrior — as Bhishma defines it in Udyoga Parva." |
| Kurukshetra | Not "the great war" or "the battle" — "Kurukshetra" or "the eighteen-day war at Kurukshetra." |
| Dharma | Never translated as just "duty." Always contextualized: "dharma — which the text treats as right action in context, not fixed rule." |

## Tone Rules

1. **State the claim before the evidence.** "Bhishma rated Karna as Ardha-Rathi — here's the section of Udyoga Parva where that appears." Not "In Udyoga Parva, there is a section where Bhishma... and this is relevant because..."

2. **Never say "some scholars believe" without naming them.** If you don't have a name, say "the text records" or "I read it as." Own your position.

3. **Correct gently, once.** If the viewer's understanding is shaped by the TV show, name that: "The 1988 Doordarshan adaptation changes this scene. The BORI text records it differently." Say it once. Don't repeat.

4. **The text doesn't moralize — and neither do we.** Report what the text records. The moral weight is the viewer's to carry.

5. **No hedging on the BORI record.** If the BORI text says X, say X. Don't soften it with "some interpretations suggest."

# PART 3 — New Language (Signature Phrases + Tone Rules)

*This is what Vyasa's Witness sounds like. These phrases should appear regularly — they are the channel's vocabulary.*

## Signature Phrases

| Phrase | Use Case |
|--------|----------|
| **"The BORI text records..."** | Introducing a fact from the source. Every factual claim starts here. |
| **"From the source."** | Closing line in the hook. Channel tagline. Use at end of intro. |
| **"What [Parva] actually says is..."** | Correcting a misconception. Always follow with the specific parva. |
| **"The text doesn't moralize — it records."** | Philosophical position. Use when discussing morally complex scenes. |
| **"Not opinion. The text."** | Emphasis closer. Use after stating a controversial but text-grounded claim. |
| **"Parva, section, description link."** | Citation shorthand in video. Use every time you cite a fact. |
| **"If you've always thought... this video will change that."** | Hook construction. Use the viewer's assumption as the setup. |
| **"Vyasa's Witness."** | Channel sign-off. "My name is Vyasa's Witness. This is what the text says." |

## Tone Calibration

**BBC History Meets Carl Sagan:**
- BBC History: measured, authoritative, never sensational — the narration leads, the imagery supports
- Carl Sagan: wonder-adjacent, slightly philosophical, able to hold intellectual weight without losing the lay audience

**What this sounds like in practice:**
- "The text records something remarkable here — not because it is dramatic, but because it is precise."
- "Bhishma is not a villain in the BORI text. But he is not innocent either. The text holds both."
- "This is a chapter the TV show skipped. I understand why. It is complicated."

**What this does NOT sound like:**
- "Oh my god, you won't BELIEVE what the Mahabharata actually says about Karna!" ← never
- "Most people don't know this secret history of..." ← never
- "The TRUTH about Draupadi that historians HIDE" ← never
