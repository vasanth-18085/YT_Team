**Content:** Short-to-medium mythology/history across Greek, Roman, Egyptian, Norse, Indian traditions. Illustrated style with AI-generated/stylised art. High volume (3,100+ videos) with consistent format.

**What to learn:**
- Thumbnail formula: character face + high-contrast text + single bold claim
- Title formula: "The [event/person] That [consequence]" — curiosity gap + historical weight
- Consistency of visual style builds channel identity fast
- Best-performing topics: lesser-known angles on famous characters, "what really happened" corrections, relationship dynamics

**What to do differently:**
- SeeUinHistory = volume (3,100 videos) at 5–15 min. Vyasa's Witness = depth (25–40 min), fewer videos, stronger retention.
- Their Indian content is surface mythology. BORI-sourced content is the scholarly version they physically cannot do.
- They cover 10 traditions. We own one. Depth beats breadth for niche authority.

**Learn from content:** Their philosophical depth on Karna, Draupadi, Yudhishthira — the level of nuance they bring. Study their intellectual approach. Ignore the Hindi-first production choice.
