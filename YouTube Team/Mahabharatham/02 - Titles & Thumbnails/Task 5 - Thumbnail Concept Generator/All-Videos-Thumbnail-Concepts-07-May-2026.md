# Task 5 — Thumbnail Concept Generator
## Vyasa's Witness | Phase 1 Launch Videos (V01–V08)

> **Date:** 2026-05-07
> **Framework:** 5C (Composition · Context · Clean · Curiosity · Color)
> **Brand palette:** Deep indigo, ochre gold (#B8860B), burnt sienna, ash white
> **Style:** Painterly, epic — Mughal miniature meets cinematic concept art. NO photorealistic faces.

---

## 5C Framework (Quick Reference)

| C | Requirement | Quality Gate |
|---|------------|-------------|
| **Composition** | Subject clear, framing intentional, eye pulled to focal point | Does it work as a 120px wide thumbnail? |
| **Context** | Passes the 18% glance test — identity clear in under 2 seconds | If someone sees this at a glance, do they know what it's about? |
| **Clean** | ≤ 3 visual elements (image + text overlay + optional accent) | No clutter. Every element earns its space. |
| **Curiosity** | Creates a knowledge gap — viewer wants to know the answer | Does this thumbnail ask a question worth answering? |
| **Color** | Brand accent dominant — deep indigo + ochre gold (#B8860B) | Does the color feel like Vyasa's Witness? |

**Text overlay rule:** Max 6 words. High contrast. Works at small size. Canva/equivalent.

## V01 — "Karna Had No Choice — And That's the Point"

### Concept A (Recommended)
**Visual:** Karna alone on a battlefield at dusk. No face visible — seen from behind or in silhouette. Golden armor catching the last light. In the distance, the Pandava banner on the horizon. Atmosphere of inevitability, not defeat.
**Text overlay:** `KARNA HAD NO CHOICE`
**Color:** Deep indigo sky, ochre gold armor glow, burnt sienna earth
**Style prefix:** Mughal miniature painting meets cinematic concept art. Epic scale. Melancholic light.

**5C Check:**
- Composition ✅ — Silhouette with golden armor as focal point. Eye moves to the banner in the distance.
- Context ✅ — Karna's armor is recognizable. Battlefield context is immediate.
- Clean ✅ — 3 elements: silhouette + golden glow + text.
- Curiosity ✅ — "Had no choice" on a warrior alone = what happened to him?
- Color ✅ — Indigo + gold = brand-perfect.

### Concept B (Alternative)
**Visual:** Two paths diverging — one lit in gold (Pandava side), one in shadow (Duryodhana's camp). Between them, a single figure standing still (Karna), armor glowing. No face.
**Text overlay:** `NO CHOICE`
**Notes:** More abstract. Concept A is recommended for launch.

## V02 — "Bhishma's Oath Was Not Noble — It Was Selfish"

### Concept A (Recommended)
**Visual:** Bhishma on the bank of the Ganga, the river behind him. He has just taken his oath — arms raised, but the gesture reads as triumphant ego rather than sacrifice. River flowing. The scene is epic but the mood is wrong. Painterly, no face.
**Text overlay:** `NOT NOBLE. SELFISH.`
**Color:** River in cool indigo-silver, the oath-taker in warm ochre-orange contrast

**5C Check:**
- Composition ✅ — Figure by water, dramatic gesture, river context.
- Context ✅ — River + warrior oath = Bhishma. Known iconography.
- Clean ✅ — Figure + river + text. Three elements.
- Curiosity ✅ — "Not noble. Selfish." directly contradicts what viewers were told.
- Color ✅ — Cool/warm contrast makes it pop.

### Concept B
**Visual:** Close up on clasped hands making an oath, with the Ganga in the background blurred. Text centered across the hands.
**Text overlay:** `SELFISH OATH`

## V03 — "Bhishma Called Karna Ardha-Rathi — The BORI Record"

### Concept A (Recommended)
**Visual:** An ancient scroll or manuscript page with Sanskrit text — partially illuminated, ochre and indigo. Overlaid: a warrior ranking chart (simple graphic showing tiers). One tier circled/highlighted in gold: "Ardha-Rathi." The identity is implied — the ranking speaks.
**Text overlay:** `HALF A WARRIOR`
**Color:** Manuscript ochre-gold tones, deep indigo background

**5C Check:**
- Composition ✅ — Manuscript texture + ranking chart as visual anchor.
- Context ✅ — Sanskrit manuscript = BORI credibility signal. Ranking chart = topic.
- Clean ✅ — Manuscript + chart + text.
- Curiosity ✅ — "Half a warrior" = who? Why? Immediately pulls the click.
- Color ✅ — Ochre gold on indigo = brand signature.

### Concept B
**Visual:** Two warriors facing each other (silhouettes — Bhishma and Karna identifiable by armor/bow style). Bhishma pointing. Karna's silhouette smaller. Battlefield backdrop.
**Text overlay:** `BHISHMA vs KARNA: THE RANKING`
**Notes:** Concept A is more distinctive. Concept B is safer/more expected.

## V04 — "Dwarka: What the BORI Mahabharata Actually Records"

### Concept A (Recommended)
**Visual:** A city sinking beneath dark ocean water, seen from above at an angle. Towers and palace architecture just visible beneath the surface, illuminated by an eerie underwater glow. Cinematic, atmospheric. No human figures.
**Text overlay:** `DWARKA: WHAT THE TEXT SAYS`
**Color:** Deep blue-indigo water, ochre-gold glow from submerged architecture, white foam

**5C Check:**
- Composition ✅ — Submerging city creates vertical depth. Eye moves down into the water.
- Context ✅ — Submerged city = Dwarka myth is widely known. Immediate recognition.
- Clean ✅ — Submerged city + light glow + text. Three elements.
- Curiosity ✅ — "What the text says" promises the source answer to a known mystery.
- Color ✅ — Dark indigo ocean + ochre glow = distinctive, brand-adjacent.

### Concept B
**Visual:** Dwarka city at its peak from a distance — golden towers, ocean, sunset. Then a crack in the ground in the foreground, suggesting imminent collapse.
**Text overlay:** `WHAT DID BORI SAY ABOUT DWARKA?`
**Notes:** Concept A (the submersion) is more dramatic and mysterious. Preferred.

## V05 — "Yudhishthira — The Most Dangerous Man in the Epic"

### Concept A (Recommended)
**Visual:** Yudhishthira at the dice game table. Not panic — cold calm. He is placing a bet. His face turned slightly away (or absent). Other figures visible but blurred in the background. The dice are gold. The table is being destroyed by the weight of his choices.
**Text overlay:** `MOST DANGEROUS MAN`
**Color:** Dark court interior, single shaft of ochre light on the dice, deep shadow on Yudhishthira's form

**5C Check:**
- Composition ✅ — Dice game setting is immediately recognizable. Focus on the cold calm.
- Context ✅ — Dice table = Sabha Parva = audience knows the reference.
- Clean ✅ — Figure + dice + text. Strong contrast.
- Curiosity ✅ — "Most dangerous" applied to the dharma king is counterintuitive — demands explanation.
- Color ✅ — Dark court + gold dice = brand palette.

### Concept B
**Visual:** Yudhishthira standing alone on a battlefield after the war. Crown on head. Everyone else dead. He is the last one standing — but the atmosphere is grief, not triumph.
**Text overlay:** `THE MOST DANGEROUS MAN`
**Notes:** Concept A (dice game) has stronger BORI-event grounding and more immediate recognition.

## V06 — "Karna vs Arjuna — Every Encounter the Text Records"

### Concept A (Recommended)
**Visual:** Two great bows on opposite sides of the frame — one clearly Karna's (larger, more ornate), one Arjuna's (iconic Gandiva silhouette). Between them, arrows in flight, frozen in mid-air. Kurukshetra dust and red-orange sky. No bodies, no faces. Pure weapons.
**Text overlay:** `KARNA vs ARJUNA: THE FULL RECORD`
**Color:** Red-orange sky, indigo dust, gold armor glints on both bows

**5C Check:**
- Composition ✅ — Split composition: Karna's bow (left) vs Arjuna's bow (right). Perfect visual symmetry for a "vs" video.
- Context ✅ — Bows + battlefield = war + characters. Instantly identifiable by Gandiva shape.
- Clean ✅ — Two bows + arrows in flight + text. Strong.
- Curiosity ✅ — "Full record" promises the definitive answer to a famous debate.
- Color ✅ — Warm red sky with brand indigo dust.

### Concept B
**Visual:** A BORI manuscript page with two warrior names in Sanskrit, both circled. A scholar's annotation marks.
**Text overlay:** `WHO ACTUALLY WON?`
**Notes:** Concept A is more cinematic and shareable. Preferred.

## V07 — "Eklavya Returns in Drona Parva — Bhima Kills Him"

### Concept A (Recommended)
**Visual:** A lone archer in forest undergrowth, thumb visibly missing from his right hand (subtle but visible). He is armed, standing, ready for war — not the helpless student he's usually depicted as. Dawn light filtering through dense trees. Atmospheric.
**Text overlay:** `BHIMA KILLED EKLAVYA`
**Color:** Deep forest greens, dawn ochre light, shadow on the handless-thumb detail

**5C Check:**
- Composition ✅ — Archer in forest creates depth. The missing thumb is the visual hook.
- Context ✅ — Archer + forest + missing thumb = Eklavya. The most famous thumb in mythology.
- Clean ✅ — Figure in forest + dawn light + text.
- Curiosity ✅ — "Bhima killed Eklavya" directly contradicts what everyone assumed (that the story ended with the thumb).
- Color ✅ — Forest + dawn light hits the brand tones.

### Concept B
**Visual:** A warrior falling in battle, with another warrior standing over him. Identifiable only by the fallen warrior's bow style (Eklavya) and the standing warrior's mace (Bhima).
**Text overlay:** `EKLAVYA'S REAL END`
**Notes:** Concept A is more distinctive and emotionally resonant. Concept B is a fallback.

## V08 — "Mahabharata — Adi Parva | Episode 1: The Beginning"

### Concept A (Recommended)
**Visual:** A great river at dawn — the Ganga or Sarasvati — with ancient palaces and temples just visible on the distant banks. A narrator figure seated on the bank (in shadow, facing away) with an open manuscript. Scale is enormous — this is the beginning of a very long story.
**Text overlay:** `ADI PARVA · EP 1`
**Color:** Dawn pink-gold sky, indigo water, ochre stone architecture

**5C Check:**
- Composition ✅ — River as the central line guiding the eye to the distant palaces. Narrator figure in foreground.
- Context ✅ — Dawn light + river + ancient palaces = the beginning of India's greatest epic.
- Clean ✅ — River + palaces + narrator silhouette + text.
- Curiosity ✅ — "The beginning of the story" triggers the natural impulse to start from Episode 1.
- Color ✅ — Dawn palette with brand tones.

### Concept B
**Visual:** The cover of an ancient manuscript, ornate, with Sanskrit script. Opening to reveal the first line of the Adi Parva — "Ugrasravas, the son of Lomaharshana..." etc.
**Text overlay:** `THE MAHABHARATA BEGINS`
**Notes:** Concept A has more visual scale. Concept B is suitable if production aesthetic becomes more text/manuscript-focused.
