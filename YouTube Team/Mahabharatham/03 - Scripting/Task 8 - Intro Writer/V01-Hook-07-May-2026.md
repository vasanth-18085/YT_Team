## Hook Type Used: Shocking Statement + Preview

The most effective hook for a BORI Corrections / Character Study hybrid is to **open with a fact that contradicts what viewers think they know**, then **preview the argument** that follows.

---

## Hook (30 seconds — Final Draft)

> His chariot wheel was stuck in the mud of Kurukshetra.
>
> And at that moment — the moment he needed it most — the most powerful weapon he had ever learned simply... wasn't there anymore. Gone. As though he had never learned it at all.
>
> If you've always thought that was bad luck, this video will change your mind.
>
> The BORI Critical Edition — the scholarly text compiled from the oldest surviving Mahabharata manuscripts — records a chain of events going back to the day Karna was born. A chain that explains, step by step, why that stuck wheel, that forgotten weapon, that final arrow were not accidents.
>
> They were the end of something that had no other possible ending.
>
> My name is Vyasa's Witness. Everything I tell you in this video comes from the BORI text. Parva, section, verse — cited in the description.
>
> This is Karna's story. From the source.

**Duration:** ~35 seconds ✅

## Hook Type Check: 10 Types

**Primary type used: Shocking Statement** — Opens with the image of the stuck wheel and the forgotten weapon, framed as "not bad luck" = contradicts viewer assumption.

**Secondary type: Preview** — "Records a chain of events... that explains, step by step..." = previews the argument structure.

**At least 3 types tested:**

### Alternative Hook A — Question Type
> "What if everything that happened to Karna at Kurukshetra was planned from the day he was born? Not by the gods. Not by fate. By the mechanics of the world he was born into?"

**Assessment:** Good but too abstract. The Shocking Statement hook grounds the viewer in a specific moment. Preferred.

### Alternative Hook B — Storytelling Type
> "There is a moment in the Karna Parva — on the seventeenth day of the war — when a man kneels in the mud of a battlefield and feels his mind go blank. The greatest weapon he has ever learned is simply gone. And the man who taught it to him explained, years before, why this would happen."

**Assessment:** Strong. More atmospheric. Could be used if Concept A's directness feels too blunt after Vasanth's review.

### Alternative Hook C — Proof Type
> "I've spent three months reading the BORI Critical Edition cover to cover. The Adi Parva, the Udyoga Parva, the Karna Parva. And by the time I reached the death of Karna, the only coherent conclusion I had was this: the text makes his death feel inevitable from page one."

**Assessment:** This is the hook for the version where Vasanth uses their own voice (non-TTS). If the channel ever transitions to a personal voiceover format, this is the opening. For TTS launch, Concept A is better.
